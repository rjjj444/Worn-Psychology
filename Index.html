The Perspective

Fashion is never just clothing. It is the psychology of the self, made visible.

Worn Psychology sits at the intersection of fashion criticism, cultural analysis, and the psychological underpinnings of how we dress. It is a space for those who believe that what we wear is one of the most intimate forms of communication we have.

From runway deconstructions to brand strategy, this is fashion examined with both a critical eye and a deep respect for the craft.

About

The Mind
Behind It

At the intersection of fashion, psychology, and culture, I work as a fashion analyst and creative strategist — examining the stories that collections, brands, and the clothes we wear tell about who we are and who we're becoming. Having walked the runways of Paris Fashion Week, I bring both an insider's eye and an analytical mind to everything I do.

Worn Psychology was built on a simple belief: that fashion is one of the most psychologically rich forms of human expression, and it deserves to be examined with the same depth and rigor as any other art form.

Through collection analysis, brand strategy, and creative direction, I help fashion labels, artists, and creatives build identities that resonate — not just aesthetically, but psychologically.

The Approach

Every collection, every brand, every creative decision tells a story. My approach is rooted in understanding the psychological intention behind the aesthetic — and communicating that story clearly and compellingly.

The Specialization

Fashion and the arts. Independent designers, music artists, and creatives building themselves as brands. I specialize in the space where personal identity becomes public presence.

The Philosophy

What you wear is never just what you wear. It is who you are, who you want to be, and what you want the world to understand about you — before you say a single word.
